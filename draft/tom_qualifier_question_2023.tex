\noindent {\bf 1.} 
Suppose that $u(c)$ is a thrice continuously differentiable function with $u' > 0, u'' < 0$ and $\lim_{c \rightarrow 0} u'(c) = + \infty$;  that $v(\ell)$ is a thrice continuously differentiable function
with $v'> 0, v'' > 0$; and  that $\beta \in (0,1),  \delta \in (0,1), b >0,  R \leq \beta^{-1}, w > 0$.  
Read the following functional equation:

\begin{equation}\label{eqn1}
J(a,h) = \max_{a, \ell_1, \ell_2} \left\{ u(c) - v(\ell_1 + \ell_2) + \beta J \bigl(R(a+ w h \ell_1 - c), (1-\delta)  h + b \ell_2 \bigr) 
  \right\} .
\end{equation}
\medskip

\noindent{\bf a.} Please bring equation (1) to life by describing an associated intertemporal optimum problem. In addition to describing a
timing protocol,   please   interpret the variables $a, h, c,  \ell_1, \ell_2, w, R$. Also interpret $J(a,h)$.  %Please completely describe timing protocols.

\medskip
\noindent {\bf b.}. Please formulate  the part {\bf a}  problem in terms of  a Lagrangian and state  first-order necessary  conditions.

\medskip
\noindent {\bf c.} Please use your part {\bf b} results to deduce Euler equations for $c, \ell_1$, and $\ell_2$.
