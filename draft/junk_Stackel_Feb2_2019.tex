\subsection{Summary and timing protocol}

Recalling that $y_t = \bmatrix{z_t \cr x_t }$, we conclude that, given $z_0$, a recursive representation of a Stackelberg plan is
$$\EQNalign{ x_0 & = - P_{22}^{-1} P_{21} z_0 \cr
             u_t & = - F y_t \cr
             y_{t+1} & = (A - BF) y_t   . \cr}  $$
%
%Equations \Ep{bell5} and \Ep{king6x0} form a  recursive representation of a Stackelberg plan
The plan is implemented with a sequence of ``continuation Stackelberg leaders'' that respect  the following timing protocol:
\smallskip


\item{1.} At time $0$,  a Stackelberg leader takes $z_0$ as given and chooses $x_0$.

\medskip

\item{2.} At times $t \geq 0$,  a continuation Stackelberg  leader takes $(z_t, x_t)$ as given and chooses $ (u_t, z_{t+1}, x_{t+1}) $.

\medskip



\noindent Notice how we have distinguished between  continuation Stackelberg leaders who choose at times $t \geq 0$ and a Stackelberg leader who
chooses at time $t=0$.
In this timing protocol, the  forward looking vector $x_t$ that obeys \Ep{eqn:xlawforward} is part of the  state vector confronting
a continuation Stackelberg leader at times $t \geq 0$ but not  part of the state confronting the Stackelberg leader at time $t=0$.  It is  presented to a continuation leader
at time $t \geq 0$ as a promise to be kept.  The time $t$ continuation leader delivers $x_t$  by choosing $u_t, z_{t+1}, x_{t+1}$.\NFootnote{See
exercise \the\chapternum.2 for a  timing protocol that builds in time consistency.}

\item{1.} At times $t \geq 0$,  a continuation Stackelberg  leader takes $(z_t, x_t)$ as given and chooses $ (u_t, z_{t+1}, x_{t+1}) $.

\medskip

\item{2.} At time $0$,  a Stackelberg leader takes $z_0$ as given and chooses $(x_0, u_0, z_1, x_1)$.

\smallskip
\noindent Notice how we have distinguished between  continuation Stackelberg leaders who choose at times $t \geq 0$ and a Stackelberg leader who
chooses at time $t=0$.

a continuation Stackelberg leader at times $t \geq 0$ but not  part of the state confronting the Stackelberg leader at time $t=0$.  It is  presented to the continuation leader
at time $t \geq 0$ as a promise to be kept.  The time $t$ continuation leader delivers $x_t$  by choosing $u_t, z_{t+1}, x_{t+1}$.\NFootnote{See
